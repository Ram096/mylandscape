How do we implement Universal Design?

Implementing universal design involves considering the diverse needs of all users and creating products, environments, or systems that are accessible, usable, and inclusive from the start. Here are steps to help you implement universal design

Donald Norman, a renowned designer and cognitive scientist, has contributed significantly to the field of design with his principles that emphasize usability, user experience, and cognitive aspects. Here are some key principles from Donald Norman and how you can implement them in your design:

Principle Description
Affordances Clear and intuitive cues that indicate how users should interact with elements.
Signifiers Labels, icons, and visual cues that indicate the function or purpose of an element.
Mapping Logical connection between controls and their effects.
Feedback Immediate and informative responses to users' actions to reduce uncertainty.
Conceptual Model Design that aligns with users' mental models and perceptions.
Visibility Prioritize visibility of relevant information and controls.
Constraints Limit certain actions to prevent errors and guide users toward intended behaviors.
Consistency Maintain consistency in layout, terminology, and interaction patterns.
Error Prevention and Recovery Design with error prevention in mind and provide clear paths for recovery.
Explorable Interfaces Create interfaces that encourage exploration and gradual learning.
Satisfaction Consider users' emotional experiences and strive to create positive interactions.
Minimize Memory Load Reduce cognitive load by minimizing the need for memorization.
Flexibility and Efficiency of Use Design for both novice and expert users, providing shortcuts for efficiency.
